I agree with the sentiment of the article, and have not installed apps that appear to replicate a website, or don’t provide functionality I would regularly need. An example is the app that my local council produced to tell me which rubbish bins to put out each week. I don’t often need to know this, and can just look it up on the website if I need to.

A native app makes sense when it uses functionality from the phone, such as location, images/video, audio, NFC etc. that wouldn’t be as easily available from a website. Examples like Strava, payment systems (Google Pay etc.), and camera/video apps. I think this is often forgotten in the rush to have an app.

Perhaps there is a gap for an app to provide notifications from SaaS sites to mobiles, although email can do this, or integration with the sites and apps like WhatsApp.
