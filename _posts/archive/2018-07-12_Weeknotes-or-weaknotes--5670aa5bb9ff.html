Weeknotes or weaknotes?

I have been meaning to start writing weeknotes for a while now, and not got into the habit. There are loads of articles about forming a habit (like this one) that talk about doing something simple and regularly (21 times before it sticks?). I think some of that has subconsciously put me off. As someone who favours structure and detail I immediately jump into thinking about how I can start the habit and not just getting on with it. My mind clouds with thoughts of repeating tasks in a task manager, is there an app to track if I am doing is consistently (other than the task manager), and thoughts about what makes a good weeknote.

And now I realise, who cares? The real benefits I can see for me for writing weeknotes is to help me reflect on what I have achieved and thereby improving my positivity, learning from both positive and not-so-positive experiences and thinking about what is coming up next. If no-one reads them then those benefits still exist, if some people read them and get something that helps them, then that is an added bonus. So I will start writing weeknotes from tomorrow, and see how it goes. In true agile fashion I will review the benefits and value they are delivering, probably tweak the format, but most importantly have a go.

Watch this space.
