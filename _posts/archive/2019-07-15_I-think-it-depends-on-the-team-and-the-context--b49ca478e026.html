I think it depends on the team and the context. When there is something very definite that needs to be done, for example preparing for a release, then a focus can help (a sprint can help here but can feel like an artificial timebox).

Most of the time though we need to be focussing on the delivery to the customer, which does not happen at the end of every sprint. This is the main reason I think we need to focus on the value delivered in a release.

The danger is we use sprints to focus on tasks that are too low level and the team loses the point of the release to the customer. That can also lead to more micromanagement and less empowerment of the team.
