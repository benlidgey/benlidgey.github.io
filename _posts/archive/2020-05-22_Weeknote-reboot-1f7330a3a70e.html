Weeknote reboot

I haven’t done a weeknote in a long while for a number of reasons, so thought I would try to do at least some brief thoughts and things I read for each week. There might not be any explanations or rationale to keep things simple.

Interesting read, trying hard to counter any confirmation bias: https://medium.com/the-liberators/the-four-symptoms-of-zombie-scrum-f107f2e86b3f

Conversation with someone looking to move into a more active delivery role from a support role. Some marketing and web development experience. Suggested looking at User needs or UX design as ways to bring the skills together. User needs and UX design are often downplayed in my experience but the benefits are massive. Working in the public sector showed the benefits and a lot is available online from places like GDS, however that cannot just be cloned as it addresses the needs of users of Government systems.

There are various conversations about lockdown starting a reboot of “life”, such as how do we travel, working from home, and making people think about what is important. That all sounds very grand, sometimes we just need to get through today and this week.

Working from home has knackered my back I think. Sciatica down one leg. Probably not helped by sitting down too long. At least in the office I got up and walked about.

Music ranged from Robert Johnson to Pantera and Slayer and in between.

Watching Netflix Pandemic series. Interesting to see all the working that goes on tracking virus transmission and mutation. Also covering the morale debate on vaccination and whether it should be mandatory or not.

Installed Ubuntu on my laptop. Works very well, with some UI niggles that take the shine off. The most notable is that the window title bars are rather chunky meaning useful screen space is reduced. There is no easy way to reduce it.
