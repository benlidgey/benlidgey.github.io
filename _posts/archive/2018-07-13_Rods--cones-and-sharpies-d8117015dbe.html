Rods, cones and sharpies

A few weeks ago I did a lightning talk at the South Wales Agile Group on the importance of data visualisation in agile development, and also for team engagement generally. The slides are available in Google Drive, or SlideShare.

I enjoy the adrenaline rush of a lightning talk, and also that creating one means you need to focus on the point of a slide. This is a useful technique to take for any presentation, whether a lightning one or not.

I have always been curious about the psychology behind agile techniques, and this was a good opportunity to explore some of that and challenging myself at the same time.

The following week I also attended the Agile Cymru conference where there was a theme throughout of visual sketch notes as a way to digest information, record it in a quickly readable way (remember that the brain processes visuals 60000 times quicker than text), and as a way to present that information to others.

I will present my sketch notes from there at some point when I have scanned them in properly. Until then, I hope you enjoy reading the slides with the 107 million cells in each eye, and can make some sense from my sketch notes.
