Weeknote #30 Lightning at the start and maybe at the end?

This week started with running a Lightning Talk session at work. The feedback so far has been positive, with the audience liking the content and the format. There were only a few minor technical hiccups, which is always nice when dealing with multiple presentations, projectors, audio and video conferencing.

As a little side-tip: I used Lorem Picsum to download some random images to use for a rolling presentation to run in the background before the actual presentations started. It was tempting to challenge someone from the audience to do an off-the-cuff lightning talk based on the random images, but that would probably have been mean.

https://picsum.photos/200/300/?random

From that positive start to the week, things continued to get better. Everyday various plans at home and work became clearer. I have been trying to start everyday with a little quiet time to look at what needs to be done that day, and focus on a small number of prioritised tasks (in a stop starting and start finishing way).

The DevOps backlog refinement continued, with clearer definitions of goals (based on OKRs) to achieve. I still like the OKR concept as it provides clear, short to medium goals and metrics to measure the progress towards those goals. It aligns well with the open and transparent principles of Agile and DevOps.

I have also been using coggle.it to document the different Agile frameworks. It has been a useful exercise for me to learn about the different approaches, and the similarities between things like Scrum, Kanban, SaFe and LeSS. If you are interested, take a look here.

The car went in for a service too without any major issues. It also gave me a chance to ride my Brompton through the park to work on a lovely sunny day. The Brompton is a wonderful piece of engineering — simple and functional. Riding through the park is so much nicer than breathing in traffic fumes.

And now it looks like it is going to rain. And hopefully some great big, exciting thunderstorms (with lightning).

What I learned this week

Visuals are very powerful ( I already knew this, but the Lightning Talks and mind maps reiterated it).

Taking some quiet time to stop and think about what needs to be done, coupled with some simple goals to achieve is worthwhile. This can be at a personal level, daily level and weekly level. This clarity has been positive for me to focus on what is important.

A positive start to each day, and the week can be a powerful force.
