S04E01 Not really weekly

Weeknotes have become less than once a week

Weeknotes have become less than once a week. While there are no rules about them being every week, the fact mine are becoming less regular has made me think about what I want to achieve from writing weeknotes (for the sake of simplicity I will still refer to weeknotes).

For me the benefit has been for me to stop and take notice of all the things that have happened over the last week. This reflection has often led me down a path of looking for trends in what has happened. This can be a good thing, but can also hide other things I have achieved if they don’t fall into that pattern. I have always been a bit verbose, and easily start writing a lot (sometimes waffling). This makes writing weeknotes more of a task than perhaps it should be. The devil’s in the detail.

Also with the whirlwind of life these days I get to a Friday evening and have to think back on all the things that have been happening, make some sense of them and craft it into a post. I should make notes as I go along and then they will almost write themselves as succinct notes.

The engineer in me now jumps into solution mode, trying to come up with easy ways using Slack integration so I can write a quick sentence of an achievement in a Slack channel that will be added to the next weeknote, requiring a quick sanity check before publishing.

Let’s experiment.
