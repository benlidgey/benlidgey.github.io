S02E07 Being Selfish

There are more than 5 things that have happened this week, but here are some highlights.

Five things I have done this week

I wrote an article on “Measure twice, cut once” on my thoughts on the importance of measurements and metrics in any transformation. How do you know you are getting better (or worse) if you don’t know where you are starting from?

Christmas preparations started. We put up the lights outside while the weather was dry (timing it perfectly — as we finished it pee’d down). This week we also wrapped some presents which is normally left until Christmas Eve. Hopefully that will mean Christmas Eve will be more relaxing.

More DIY in bedrooms. Hoping to finish this phase this weekend.

My Mac decided to throw a hissy-fit about connecting to GitHub and SSH. In the end I went back to basics by removing all the keys and starting from scratch.

On a related note I have continued to experiment with Spring Boot and Spring Data storage. I still like the concept of Spring and as the support for functionality grows it makes sense to use it, hiding the mechanics for thing like connecting to databases or REST services, and concentrating on the business logic. The only thing that seems to get in the way are the different ways to do something, like implementing Cucumber tests, with Spring or Spring Boot.

Five things I learned this week

It has been good to get back into technical work. It is something that I have not been doing as much of as I would have liked recently, focussing more on the Agile/DevOps processes. However over the past few weeks I have been reviewing what I enjoy doing — my conclusion is that I enjoy making and delivering digital solutions which require the right mix of Agile, DevOps and technology.

As part of this review I have resigned from my current place to move to a new job that has a better mix of Agile, DevOps and tech. I have always felt like a bit guilty when going to interviews and applying for jobs. I have been pleasantly surprised by the congratulations I have had since resigning.

It is OK to be selfish sometimes. I have had feedback in the past that I can be “too nice” and “too helpful” and try to make things better for others, sometimes at my own expense. I will make a concerted effort to stop and check if I am being too nice before doing something, and to put myself higher up the priority list. I read an article somewhere that said it is OK to hate people (or something like that) — if you dislike someone then that’s fine, just be professional about it, but don’t think you have to try and be nice to them. There some slimey suckers in the world, and I won’t go out of my way to be nice and helpful to them anymore.

I cannot mitre cut wood very well, even if I have measured it twice.

Apps like https://www.justwatch.com/ can be good for searching across streaming services. It would be great to have an aggregated view of what is bookmarked in each of my streaming services. Also, streaming services look great, but given the crazy world of content rights it is annoying when I’ve bookmarked something to watch but when I get around to it, the content has expired. It makes having a DVR more appealing again.
