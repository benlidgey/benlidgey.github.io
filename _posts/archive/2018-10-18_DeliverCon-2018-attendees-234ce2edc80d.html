DeliverCon 2018 attendees

At DeliverCon 2018 we had a map at the sign in desk for people to mark where they have come from. The intention was to get a feel for the geographic location, organisations or departments and for people to identify others local to them to help networking.

At the end of the day the map looked like this:

Findings

A good spread of people from around the country (except no one from Scotland or Northern Ireland).

Longest distances are Plymouth (approx 200 miles or 3 hours 40 minutes on a train), Newcastle (approx 200 miles or 3 hours on a train),

People (presumably from London) don’t know where London is!

No indication of organisations or departments people came from.

No networking details for people to find others nearby.

No sense of the numbers of people at each location.

Links into the discussions on how do we increase the diversity of the attendees. For example, did the news about the event reach people in Scotland? Was it too far to travel?

Retrospective thoughts

The map should have some markers on for main cities so people can have a sense for where they come from.

Some way for people to identify their organisation or department, perhaps different colours? What about accessibility issues?

Some way for people to leave their contact details and location to allow discovery of others nearby. Are there any privacy concerns about doing this?

Are there any other details we would want to capture? How about preferences for the location of the next one?

Would a different format make more sense? Or a different way of capturing the information.

Do we want to find out how people travelled to the event, so we can get an idea of the different transport used and consider the environmental impact?
