Things from this week

Spring Boot and React

I have been a fan of Spring and Spring Boot for a while. I have been refreshing my knowledge and still like how easy it is to put together apps with web UIs using React, REST APIs and data storage. Despite all the tinkering and coding my focus still needs to be on designing (at least a high-level design) first. Agile doesn’t mean designs can be ignored, but that there needs to be enough design to deliver the first value to the customer.

Site Reliability Engineering

I have been reading some of the Site Reliability Engineering book online. Some of the ways of working make sense. The one that I found interesting in Chapter One was the cap on operational work at 50%. I have seen places where operational work takes over and the teams spend all their time fire-fighting and no time for fixing and improving. It sounds like a big cultural challenge to convince people that the cap makes sense as there will be fear that the service will degrade horribly unless there is someone sat there watching it.

Jira review

I have been looking at the use of Jira as it is used in many different ways by teams. This means it is difficult to get consistent progress metrics. One way to address that would be to set up each project in the same way. Teams can still estimate in their own ways, but the statuses and burn-up charts can show the actual empirical progress. However there is no way in Jira to have project templates. We would instead need to write detailed steps to create a project with a standard config — people won’t follow those steps, or maintain them, and so the work will be a waste.

Reading the “JIRA is an antipattern” article was interesting that Jira can make teams look at the minutiae of the work and not the bigger picture.

Blockchain update

Today we had an update on the Blockchain research being done by Ali at Cardiff Met. It is a complicated area with lots of potential avenues to research and offers lots of ways to challenge the current way of thinking.

Turbo trainer

I finally got around to setting up the smart turbo trainer. It is much better to have the resistance controlled, even with a basic training app that doesn't have fancy videos, just varies the resistance based on hill profiles.
